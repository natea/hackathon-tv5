## Key Teaching Moments (1-2 min each)

### Scenario 1: "I Feel" vs. "You Always"
**What Works**: "I feel invisible when you help him but not me"  
**What Doesn't**: "You always favor him"  
**Why**: Can't argue with internal experience; easier to defend against accusations

### Scenario 2: Broken Record
**What Works**: Repeat boundary without new material  
**What Doesn't**: Justify, argue, defend, explain (JADE)  
**Why**: Each explanation gives ammunition for continued debate

### Scenario 3: Gray-Rocking
**What Works**: "Okay, I hear you." (End)  
**What Doesn't**: "But X happened! You said Y!"  
**Why**: Removes fuel for debate; boring responses end interrogation

### Scenario 4: Need vs. Neediness
**Healthy Need**: Want basic connection with parent; knowing you're worthy  
**Unhealthy Neediness**: Can't function without frequent contact; guilt-trip  
**Your Situation**: You're in healthy category; his limitation ‚â† your flaw

### Scenario 5: Systems Thinking
**The Role**: Family needs scapegoat for dysfunction  
**The Truth**: Scapegoating serves psychological purpose for family  
**The Choice**: Keep role (enmeshed) or reject it (face family anger)  
**The Work**: Grief + agency, not fixing them
